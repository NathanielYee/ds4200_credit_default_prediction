Loan Percent Income vs Loan Interest Rate

The scatter plot aboves explores the relationship between the percentage of income dedicated to loans and the corresponding loan interest rates.

scatter plot reveals compelling insights about the relationship between loan percent income and interest rates across various loan types. A clear negative correlation emerges, where higher percentages of income dedicated to loans generally correspond with lower interest rates, suggesting that borrowers who can commit more income to loan payments often secure better terms. Interest rates span from roughly 5% to 22%, with most loans clustering between 6% and 16%, while borrowers typically allocate between 0-50% of their income to loans, with the highest concentration in the 0-40% range. Notably, the pattern remains consistent across all loan types (Personal, Education, Medical, Venture, Home Improvement, and Debt Consolidation), indicating that loan purpose may not significantly influence interest rates. The data shows a prominent clustering in the 5-15% interest rate range when loan payments represent 20-40% of income, suggesting this range represents an optimal balance for both lenders and borrowers. This analysis implies that borrowers might benefit from structuring their loans to represent a larger portion of their income to secure better rates, though they should be cautious about exceeding the 50% threshold. The consistency across loan types suggests that borrowers should prioritize their income-to-loan ratio over loan type when seeking favorable rates.
